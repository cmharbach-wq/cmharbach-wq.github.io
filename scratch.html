Why Mental Health Matters to Me

Connection is one of the most powerful forms of healing.

Mental health is talked about more than ever — but for millions of people, access to real support still feels out of reach. The stigma is too high. The cost is too high. The wait is too long. And often, people simply don't know where to start.

I've seen this gap up close: in friends who didn't know how to ask for help, in family members who waited too long, in myself on the hard days when "just push through it" didn't cut it. I believe we can do better — not just as individuals, but as communities.

That's why I've started thinking seriously about building a nonprofit organization dedicated to making mental health resources more accessible, less intimidating, and more human. Not another app that disappears from your home screen. Something real, rooted in community and genuine care.

My Vision for Change

If I were to launch a nonprofit tomorrow, these are the pillars it would be built on. Each one addresses a different layer of the problem — because real change requires tackling it from more than one angle.

  1. Community-First Support Networks
    • Trained peer mentors in schools, universities, and workplaces
    • Safe, moderated online communities for people who aren't ready for therapy
    • Regular in-person events to reduce isolation and build belonging
  2. Education & Stigma Reduction
    • Free curriculum on emotional literacy for K–12 schools
    • Workshops for parents, coaches, and educators on recognizing mental distress
    • Public storytelling campaigns to normalize asking for help
  3. Accessible Professional Resources
    • Sliding-scale therapy referral network for uninsured or underinsured individuals
    • Partnerships with clinics to reduce wait times through telehealth options
    • Crisis intervention hotline staffed by trained volunteers
  4. Technology That Actually Helps
    • A curated, evidence-based app library — no gimmicks, no monetized anxiety
    • SMS-based check-in tools for people without smartphones or reliable internet
    • AI-assisted journaling prompts designed with licensed therapists

Community is not a buzzword — it's the foundation of healing.

What You Can Do Right Now

If you're struggling:

You are not alone. These resources are free and confidential — reaching out is an act of strength, not weakness.

  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline — Call or text 988 anytime
  • Crisis Text Line — Text HOME to 741741
  • NAMI Helpline — 1-800-950-NAMI (6264)
  • BetterHelp / Talkspace — Affordable online therapy

If you want to help:

  • Listen without judgment when someone opens up to you
  • Volunteer with or donate to NAMI, TWLOHA, or local mental health nonprofits
  • Share this page — awareness is the first step toward change
  • Take the quiz and share it with someone you care about
