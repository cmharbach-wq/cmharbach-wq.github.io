Why This Matters

The help is real. The access isn't.

Sometimes what helps most is just someone showing up.

People talk about mental health more than they used to, which is a good thing. But actually getting help is still way harder than it should be. Therapy is expensive, waitlists are long, and if you don't know where to start, you usually don't start at all.

I've watched this happen to friends, family, and honestly myself on the hard days. "Just push through" doesn't really cut it when your brain is working against you.

That's why I've been thinking about starting a nonprofit someday. Not another app that lives on your home screen for a week, but something that fits into people's actual lives.

What I'd Build

Four things. All of them, at once.

If I could start a nonprofit tomorrow, these are the four things I'd want it to focus on. None of them fix the whole problem alone, but together I think they could actually help.

  1. Community First
    • Peer mentors in schools, colleges, and workplaces
    • Moderated online spaces for people not ready for therapy yet
    • In-person events so people feel less alone
  2. Education & Less Stigma
    • Free emotional-literacy lessons for K–12 classrooms
    • Workshops for parents, coaches, and teachers on what to look for
    • Honest storytelling campaigns that make asking for help feel normal
  3. Real Professional Resources
    • A sliding-scale therapy network for people without great insurance
    • Telehealth partnerships so waitlists stop being a deal-breaker
    • A crisis line staffed by trained volunteers
  4. Tech That Actually Helps
    • A curated list of apps that are actually backed by research
    • SMS check-ins for people without great phones or internet
    • Journaling prompts designed with real therapists, not just an algorithm

Community isn't filler to me. It's the part most efforts skip.

What You Can Do

If you want to do something.

If you're the one struggling:

You're not alone, and reaching out doesn't make you weak. These are free, confidential, and made for exactly this.

  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline. Call or text 988 anytime.
  • Crisis Text Line. Text HOME to 741741.
  • NAMI Helpline. 1-800-950-NAMI (6264).
  • BetterHelp / Talkspace. Online therapy that's easier to start with.

If you want to help someone else:

  • Actually listen when someone opens up, without jumping to advice
  • Donate or volunteer with NAMI, TWLOHA, or a local nonprofit
  • Share this page with someone it might reach
  • Take the quiz and send it to a friend who needs a nudge
